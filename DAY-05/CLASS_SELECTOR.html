Top 10 Books in the World

  1. The Great Gatsby by F. Scott Fitzgerald

    "The Great Gatsby" is a classic novel that explores themes of wealth, love, and the American Dream in the 1920s.

  2. To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

    "To Kill a Mockingbird" is a powerful novel that addresses issues of racism and social injustice in the American South.

  3. 1984 by George Orwell

    "1984" is a dystopian novel that delves into themes of totalitarianism, censorship, and surveillance in a bleak future society.

  4. Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen

    "Pride and Prejudice" is a classic romance novel that explores societal norms and the complexities of relationships in the 19th century.

  5. Don Quixote by Miguel de Cervantes

    "Don Quixote" is an epic novel that follows the adventures of an idealistic, albeit delusional, knight-errant.

  6. The Catcher in the Rye by J.D. Salinger

    "The Catcher in the Rye" is a coming-of-age novel that delves into the struggles of its young protagonist, Holden Caulfield.

  7. The Bible

    The Bible is a religious and historical text with deep cultural significance, containing stories, teachings, and poetry central to various faiths.

  8. War and Peace by Leo Tolstoy

    "War and Peace" is an epic novel that explores the lives of several aristocratic Russian families during the Napoleonic era.

  9. Crime and Punishment by Fyodor Dostoevsky

    "Crime and Punishment" is a psychological novel that delves into the moral dilemmas of its tormented protagonist, Raskolnikov.

  10. The Lord of the Rings by J.R.R. Tolkien

    "The Lord of the Rings" is a fantasy epic that follows the journey of a hobbit, Frodo Baggins, as he seeks to destroy a powerful ring and save Middle-earth.
